Introduction

Vidya Hi Sewa

Lady Irwin College is a constituent college for women, in the University of Delhi. Established in 1932, it is a premiere institution for Undergraduate and Postgraduate education in Home Science, supporting doctoral programs in five specialized areas.

The College motto — Vidya Hi Sewa (Service through Knowledge) — was bestowed by Mahatma Gandhi himself. True to this vision, the teaching-learning experience strives to inculcate a sense of knowledge to serve through carefully designed outreach experiences.

The academic disciplines are artistic, creative, culturally rooted and contemporary — scientifically planned to include education in textile technology, food processing, environment, sustainable technologies, food safety, health and human development.

Our Legacy

A Heritage of
Excellence

From a modest beginning with 11 students in 1932, Lady Irwin College has provided higher education for generations of women. Well into the ninth decade, the college now has over 1,300 students every year.

The college was established under the patronage of Lady Dorothy Irwin, by eminent leaders including the Maharanis of Baroda and Bhopal, Sarojini Naidu, Rajkumari Amrit Kaur, Annie Besant, and Kamala Devi Chattopadhyay.

The buildings of the college campus have been classified and protected as heritage sites — a testament to both architectural and academic significance that has guided over 200 colleges across India and neighbouring countries.

Our Purpose

Vision & Mission

"Strives to inculcate the spirit of service along with professional development and skills for women empowerment through state of the art education, research and extension by nurturing innovation, leadership and national development."

Lady Irwin College has been a pioneer in women's education. Set up more than eight decades ago, the vision for empowering women continues to be the key thrust of the College. The college remains committed to building leadership, conscious citizenry and active participation of women for furthering national developmental goals.

Eminent national and international leaders helped envision Lady Irwin College goals and the role it could play in the field of higher education for women. They built strong foundations based on core values of social justice, veracity, service and sustainability for achieving excellence in all spheres of life.

The college faculty has consistently strived to contemporize its academic content through innovative research, strong community outreach and implementation of new technological knowledge. Every department addresses larger societal issues like health, gender, conservation of textile heritage, and education.

Through curricular and co-curricular activities, we strive to:

  • Accomplish training and development of young women for professional employment
  • Generate appreciation and respect for our cultural heritage and traditions
  • Undertake training of trainers and educators
  • Develop research and critical analysis skills for national development strategies
  • Strengthen linkages with teaching and research institutions globally
  • Give impetus to community outreach and extension
